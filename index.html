ABOUT ME

ZAHA HADID

I graduated in 1977 from the Architectural Association in London, worked as a teaching assistant at the College of Architecture in 1987, and organized as a visiting professor at several universities in Europe and America, including Harvard, Chicago, Hamburg, Ohio, Columbia, New York and Yale.
I adhered to the deconstructive school of style and modernity in design, and implemented 950 projects in 44 countries. My work is characterized by imagination, as I place my designs in free, loose lines that are not defined by horizontal or vertical lines. It was also distinguished by its durability. The projects of the fire station in Germany in 1993, the Italian Art Museum building in Rome in 2009 and the American in Cincinnati, Abu Dhabi Bridge, the London Marine Sports Center, which was dedicated to the Olympic Games in 2012, the subway station in Strasbourg, the cultural center of Azerbaijan The scientific center in Walesburg, the steamboat station in Salerino, the ice skating center in Innsbruck and the Heydar Aliyev Cultural Center in Baku in 2013 are among the most prominent projects that have deservedly brought me to the world stage.
I received many prestigious awards, medals and honorary titles in the arts of architecture, and I was one of the first women to receive the Pritzker Prize in Architecture in 2004, which is equivalent in value to the Nobel Prize in Engineering; the Stirling Prize on two occasions; She was awarded the Order of the British Empire and the Japanese Imperial Medal in 2012.
